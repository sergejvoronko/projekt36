## How Air Suspension Works on an E36

The BMW E36 did not come with air suspension from the factory. Every air ride setup is aftermarket, replacing the factory struts/shocks and springs with air struts (shock absorbers with air bags instead of coil springs).

A complete air ride system has four main components:

**Air struts** — one per corner. These are the actual suspension components that replace your factory setup. The air bag inflates to raise the car and deflates to lower it, while the integrated shock absorber provides damping.

**Compressor** — an electric air compressor that fills a tank with compressed air. Mounted in the trunk.

**Air tank** — stores compressed air so you don't have to wait for the compressor every time you adjust height. Mounted in the trunk.

**Management system** — the controller that routes air to each strut. This is where kits differ most dramatically — from basic manual valves to fully digital, app-controlled, height-sensing systems.

## Kit Breakdown

### Air Lift Performance (3P and 3H)

Air Lift is the market leader for performance air suspension. Their E36 kit uses threaded-body, monotube struts that provide the best ride quality and handling of any bolt-on air kit.

**3P (Pressure-based):** Uses pressure sensors to monitor and maintain a set PSI at each corner. You set your desired pressure, and the system maintains it. Simpler, more affordable, and works well for most users. The main drawback: ride height changes with load (add passengers and the car sits lower).

**3H (Height + Pressure):** Adds height sensors to each corner. You set your desired ride height in millimeters, and the system adjusts air pressure automatically to maintain that height regardless of load. More expensive but more precise.

Both systems include a digital controller with up to 5 presets (e.g., "drive," "show," "loading," etc.), Bluetooth app control, and the option for a wired paddle controller.

**Pros:**
- Best ride quality of any bolt-on kit
- Threaded body allows mechanical height adjustment beyond air range
- 30-level damping adjustment
- Excellent customer support and documentation
- Wide dealer network including EU distributors

**Cons:**
- Most expensive option
- EU buyers pay import duties + shipping (~€150–200 extra)
- Complete kit with management is a significant investment

**EU availability:** Air Lift ships to Europe through distributors. Check Lowered Lifestyle, Stance Parts EU, or order direct. Budget for customs duties (~20% + VAT on the declared value).

### Bag Riders (Manual and PRO)

Bag Riders is a US-based company that builds custom air ride kits. Their E36 kit uses their own strut assemblies paired with different management systems.

**Manual kit (~€2,200–2,800):** The simplest system. Uses pneumatic paddle valves to manually fill/dump each air bag. No electronics, no controller, no presets. You fill the bags with a switch and adjust by feel. Incredibly reliable because there's almost nothing to break.

**PRO kit (~€3,800–4,500):** Their digital management system with a controller, presets, and Bluetooth capability. Similar in concept to the Air Lift 3P but at a slightly lower price point.

**Pros:**
- Manual kit is the most reliable option (no electronics to fail)
- Good strut quality
- Excellent customer service and build-to-order approach
- Lower price point than Air Lift for comparable features

**Cons:**
- Ships from the US — expect 2–3 weeks shipping + customs
- Manual kit has no presets — every adjustment is by feel
- Less EU support infrastructure than Air Lift

### D2 Racing Air Struts

D2 Racing offers air struts as standalone components — you buy the struts and pair them with your own management system. This is a good option if you want to build a custom setup or if you already have a management system.

**Pros:**
- 36-way damping adjustment (excellent tuneability)
- Direct bolt-on replacement for E36
- Can be paired with any management system
- Available through EU distributors (AccuAir EU, various stance shops)
- Lower cost when buying struts only

**Cons:**
- No complete kit — you need to source management, tank, compressor, lines, and fittings separately
- Building your own system requires more knowledge and planning
- Quality control reports are mixed — some users report inconsistent damping valve feel

---

### CEIKA Air Ride Coilovers

CEIKA takes a different approach: they start with a full adjustable coilover and replace the spring with an air bag. This gives you the best of both worlds — the handling precision of a threaded coilover body with the height adjustability of air.

**Pros:**
- 36-click damping adjustment on every corner
- Full threaded body for mechanical height range
- Ride quality is very close to a good coilover — firmer than pure air struts but more controlled
- Available in multiple management configurations (manual, 2-way, 4-way)

**Cons:**
- Ships from Asia — lead time is 2–4 weeks
- Higher price for the premium management options
- Less brand recognition in the EU air ride community
- Bags sit on the coilover body — different aesthetic when components are visible

---

### DOLUD (Budget Option)

DOLUD is a Polish company that produces budget-friendly E36 air ride struts. For EU buyers, they're the most accessible option with no customs issues (EU-based) and fast shipping.

**Pros:**
- Cheapest struts available (~€800–1,200)
- Based in Poland — ships within EU, no customs
- Bolt-on fitment
- Aluminium construction, rebuildable

**Cons:**
- Struts only — no management system included
- Ride quality is basic — limited damping adjustment
- Less documentation and community support
- Best suited for show builds that don't see daily driving

## What You Need Beyond Struts

If you buy struts-only (D2, DOLUD, or CEIKA bare kit), you'll need to source these separately:

| Component | What to look for | Est. cost |
|---|---|---|
| Air compressor | Viair 444C is the standard. Dual compressor for faster fill. | €150–250 |
| Air tank | 2.5 or 5 gallon aluminium. Bigger = more reserve, fewer compressor cycles. | €80–150 |
| Management system | 4-way solenoid valve manifold + controller. AccuAir e-Level, Air Lift 3P/3H standalone, or manual paddle valves. | €300–2,000 |
| Air line | 1/4" DOT-rated air line. Buy 15–20 meters. | €30–50 |
| Fittings | Push-to-connect fittings for all connections. Count every connection point. | €40–60 |
| Wiring | Power kit for compressor (relay, fuse, wiring) | €20–40 |
| Mounting hardware | Tank mounts, compressor mounts, line routing clips | €20–30 |

**Budget management build total:** ~€650–850 on top of strut cost
**Premium management build total:** ~€1,500–2,500 on top of strut cost

## Trunk Setup Considerations for E36 Sedan

The trunk of the E36 sedan is your installation space for the compressor, tank, and management. Planning this layout before buying components saves headaches.

**Tank placement:** Most builds mount the tank centered in the trunk, either flat on the floor or vertically against the rear seat wall. A 2.5-gallon tank fits without sacrificing too much trunk space. A 5-gallon tank takes significantly more room.

**Compressor placement:** Mount on a solid surface — the compressor vibrates. Use rubber isolating mounts. Keep it away from the exhaust heat path. Most builders mount it next to or on top of the tank.

**Line routing:** Air lines run from the management manifold (usually near the tank) forward through the car to each corner. The lines can be routed through the factory brake line channels under the car, through the interior along the sills, or through the trunk and under the rear seat. Through the interior is cleaner but more labor-intensive.

**Spare wheel:** Most air ride builds sacrifice the spare wheel well. The tank fits perfectly in this space. Carry a tire repair kit instead.
